## Findings (hand-added post-run analysis)

1. **These are the M6 gate numbers.** The chunk arm must beat `gemini2-hybrid`
   overall r@10 **0.2684** AND its >10k band r@10 **0.3867** (and the semantic
   arm's 0.3439 / 0.3333 for the phone-profile shape), like-for-like through
   the same pipeline.

2. **Hybrid r@10 < semantic-only r@10 (0.2684 vs 0.3439), while hybrid wins
   r@1/MRR (0.143/0.174 vs 0.107/0.153).** The keyword channel sharpens the
   head (dual-channel agreement) but its candidates displace semantic-only
   golds from ranks 4–10 — worst in the <6k band (0.188 vs 0.350). In the
   >10k band the inversion flips: hybrid 0.3867 > semantic 0.3333 — the
   full-text keyword scan reaches content past the 10k embed truncation.

3. **Miss decomposition (probe, 50 random gemini2-semantic misses re-scored
   against the raw unscoped store):** gold's raw semantic rank was ≤10 for
   27/50 and ≤40 (inside the candidate window) for 31/50; only 15/50 were
   beyond rank 200 (genuine embedding misses). The dominant failure mode of
   this baseline is **rank-space displacement, not embedding quality**: after
   RRF, the entire 40-candidate relevance span is 1/61−1/100 ≈ 0.0064, while
   the recency tie-break adds up to 0.05 — ~8× the whole span — so fresh
   candidates leapfrog older golds wholesale. The retrieval.py docstring claim
   that weight=0.05 "can NEVER overturn a clearly-better older match" holds on
   cosine-scale scores but NOT post-RRF. The labeled set (spread across age
   quartiles) exposes what the golden set (recent-work queries, dual-channel
   golds) does not. Candidate fix (recency weight expressed in rank-space
   units, or applied pre-fusion) is OUT OF SCOPE for M4 — flagged for the
   plan; the M6 comparison is unaffected because both arms run the identical
   pipeline.

4. **qwen3-0.6b covered-only r@10 0.3429 ≈ gemini-2 semantic 0.3439** (MRR
   lower: 0.145 vs 0.153). The local model is competitive at r@10 on this
   task. 51/503 golds are uncovered in its store (sample-level view of the
   ~600-snapshot store gap; the missing snapshots skew recent).

5. **Holdout: 3/3 hits on every arm** (hybrid ranks all three #1 — the
   dual-channel + recency shape these human queries were designed around).
